About Me

Researcher · Engineer · Builder

I am a PhD researcher in the Cyber Physical Networking (CPN) Lab at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, advised by Dr. Mehmet Can Vuran. My research focuses on environment-adaptive mobile wireless systems for 6G, with channel modeling and beam management across rural and urban environments.

I design and build experimental testbeds using software-defined radios (USRP X300/B200) and 60 GHz phased arrays, develop wideband channel sounding systems with Zadoff–Chu sequences, and investigate camera-primed beam management strategies for vehicular mmWave networks. My work bridges the gap between theoretical channel models and real-world propagation measurements collected across agricultural fields, rural highways, and indoor environments.

I am targeting research engineer and RF engineering roles where I can apply my expertise in wireless system design, signal processing, and data-driven optimization to build next-generation communication systems.

Research Highlights

Primary research thrusts in wireless systems

Camera-Primed mmWave Beam Management (ViBe)

Engineered a hybrid closed-loop, hardware-agnostic beam alignment framework for real-time vehicular mmWave communication. ViBe fuses camera-based scene understanding with model-driven beam initialization and online SNR-driven refinement, reducing beam search from O(N²) to constrained local search. Validated across indoor/outdoor testbeds and real-time vehicular experiments, achieving 1.1–1.4% outage rates—outperforming 5G NR hierarchical beamforming and state-of-the-art end-to-end ML models.

mmWave Beamforming V2X Computer Vision 6G
IEEE SECON 2026 (submitted)

mmWave Agricultural Channel Modeling

Led the most extensive mmWave agricultural channel measurement campaign to date, collecting 31,600+ data points in corn and soybean fields across two growing seasons at three farm sites. Developed the mmW-Ag-MLR channel model using multivariate regression with weather parameters (wind, temperature, humidity, vapor concentration, solar radiation) and crop-biological features (plant height, leaf area index). Demonstrated that crop canopy acts as a secondary reflective surface increasing path-loss exponents across growth stages.

Channel Modeling 60 GHz Propagation Agriculture 6G Ag-IoT
IEEE TWC (submitted)

Wideband Rural Channel Sounding

Implemented a correlation-based wideband channel sounding architecture using zero-padded Zadoff–Chu sequences and USRP X300 radios at 5.55 GHz. Conducted LOS and NLOS rural measurements up to 1.3 km at 50 MHz bandwidth (6 m distance resolution, 20 ns delay resolution). Built a complete calibration and signal-processing pipeline: detection, coherent averaging, back-to-back calibration, hardware deconvolution, CIR reconstruction, PDP extraction, and path-loss regression in Python/CuPy.

USRP X300 Zadoff–Chu Channel Sounding Rural Sub-6 GHz

Selected Projects

Experimental systems from concept to field deployment

Wireless Testbed & Experimental Infrastructure

Built mmWave (60 GHz) and sub-6 GHz testbeds integrating phased arrays, USRP X300/B200 radios, power amplifiers, low-noise amplifiers, bandpass filters, and GPS-disciplined oscillator synchronization hardware. Developed custom beam-sweeping, real-time acquisition, GPU-accelerated correlation, and automated post-processing workflows for SNR, path-loss exponent, RMS delay spread, and beam consistency analysis.

USRP X300/B200 60 GHz Phased Arrays Python/CuPy GNU Radio
GitHub

ViBe: Vision-Based Beamforming Framework

Camera-primed double-directional beam management system for vehicular mmWave communication. Combines camera priming, radio coordinate projection, beam initialization, iterative SNR-driven refinement, and offset tracking to achieve real-time V2X link establishment without exhaustive beam search or offline RF training.

Python PyTorch MATLAB Wireless InSite Sionna
GitHub

Multi-Modal Traffic Pattern Detection

Built a real-time three-class traffic classification system using synchronized USRP radio-frequency signals and camera images. Implemented real-valued and complex-valued CNN models achieving >80% accuracy with <100 ms inference latency and ±10 ms synchronization for continuous operation.

USRP CNN Python Real-Time

mmWave 3D Ray Tracing Framework

Developed a MATLAB-based 3D ray tracing simulator for mmWave indoor propagation with multi-bounce reflections and log-normal shadowing. Modeled path loss and reflection loss at 24 GHz, generated power-gradient visualizations, and automated SVG/EPS and Python GIF outputs.

MATLAB Ray Tracing 24 GHz Python

Channel Sounding Signal Processing Pipeline

End-to-end calibration and signal-processing framework for wideband channel sounding data. Includes signal detection, coherent averaging, back-to-back calibration, hardware deconvolution, calibrated CIR reconstruction, power delay profile extraction, and path-loss regression—all GPU-accelerated with Python/CuPy.

Python CuPy/GPU NumPy Signal Processing
